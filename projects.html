Blair3Sat

CubeSats are miniaturized satellites which usually come in sizes of 1U to 6U, each unit measuring approximately 10 x 10 x 10 cubic centimeters, and provide an opportunity to demonstrate smaller missions. The Blair3Sat team is designing a satellite to measuring energetic particles at the boundary of the inner and outer Van Allen belts, helping to both expand space weather research in the magnetosphere and inspire other high school students to explore space. You can read more about us on our website.


ISS attitude determination

I worked with near real-time orbit data from the USGNC (U.S. Guidance, Navigation, and Control) system to determine the field of view of an instrument installed on the International Space Station. You can read my report here.


VSOP87 implementation

I implemented VSOP87 in Java using the Neoprogammatics constants generator and calculated the mean solar equinox, comparing it to Newcomb's calculations. I've uploaded the code to my GitHub.
