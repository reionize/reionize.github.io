Research

Local scrambling

While working in Prof. John Preskill's group at the Caltech IQIM, I proposed a new definition of local information scrambling for quantum states based on the maximum size of a connected subsystem for which an entangling measurement can be performed. Under certain conditions, this is achieved by random circuits in depth scaling logarithmically with the size of the region. These results has potential implications for quantum complexity and thermalization. See here for a write-up on the work.
